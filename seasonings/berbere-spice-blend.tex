\section*{Directions}

\noindent
Yield: approximately 1~cup

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Toast \textbf{fenugreek~seeds} in a dry skillet over medium-low heat, stirring constantly, for \textit{2-3~minutes} until fragrant and darkened one shade. Remove immediately when nutty, maple-like aroma develops. Transfer to a plate to cool.
    
    \item Toast \textbf{coriander~seeds} in the same skillet over medium heat, stirring frequently, for \textit{3-4~minutes} until fragrant and slightly darker. Transfer to cool.
    
    \item Toast \textbf{cumin~seeds}, \textbf{black~peppercorns}, and \textbf{allspice~berries} together over medium heat, stirring constantly, for \textit{2-3~minutes} until \textbf{cumin} becomes fragrant and darkens slightly. Transfer to cool.
    
    \item Let all toasted spices cool for \textit{5~minutes}. Grind each group separately in a spice grinder or mortar and pestle to a fine powder. \textbf{Fenugreek} is particularly hardâ€”grind until no large pieces remain.
    
    \item In a medium bowl, combine all ground toasted spices with \textbf{sweet~paprika}, \textbf{cayenne~pepper}, \textbf{ground~cardamom}, \textbf{ground~cinnamon}, \textbf{ground~ginger}, \textbf{ground~turmeric}, \textbf{ground~cloves}, and \textbf{dried~thyme}.
    
    \item Whisk thoroughly for \textit{2-3~minutes} to ensure even distribution of all spices. Optionally sift for ultra-fine texture.
    
    \item Store in an airtight container in a cool, dark place. Peak flavor within \textit{2-3~months}, still good for \textit{6~months}. Flavor mellows and marries over the first week.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Usage Notes}

\noindent
For stews and braises, use 2-3~Tbsp. per pound of meat. Bloom \textbf{berbere} in oil or butter over medium heat for \textit{1-2~minutes} before adding liquids to fully develop aromatic compounds. The spices require fat and time to release their complete flavor complexity.
